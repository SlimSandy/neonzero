1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

   Project LETHE is Omnicron Industries' flagship initiative
   in the field of Cognitive Restructuring Technology (CRT).
   Named for the river of forgetfulness in Greek mythology,
   LETHE achieves targeted erasure and replacement of human
   memory structures through a proprietary neural interface
   system.

   In simple terms: we can selectively erase memories and
   overwrite them with new ones.

2. CAPABILITIES (CURRENT)

   - Full cognitive mapping of hippocampal memory structures
   - Selective erasure of episodic and semantic memory
   - Implantation of synthetic memory templates (identities)
   - Neural pathway reconstruction for behavioral modification
   - Consciousness-level personality rewriting

3. PROGRESS TO DATE

   Phase 1 (2024-Q1 to 2024-Q4):
     Proof of concept. Animal trials. Memory erasure in rats
     achieved with 94% accuracy. Minor ethical review. Approved.

   Phase 2 (2025-Q1 to 2026-Q1):
     Human trials. 31 subjects to date.
     Results:
       - Complete memory erasure: 27/31 (87.1%)
       - Successful identity template overlay: 23/31 (74.2%)
       - Subject survival rate: 24/31 (77.4%)
       - Post-procedure behavioral stability: 19/31 (61.3%)

     NOTE: 7 subject fatalities occurred during Phase 2.
     Cause of death classified as "neural cascade failure"
     in all cases. Subjects 004, 009, 015, 017, 022, 026,
     and 029 are listed as "transferred" in official records.

   Phase 3 (2026-Q2, PROPOSED):
     Expanded subject pool. Enhanced protocols.
     Goal: 95% success rate with zero mortality.
     Projected subjects: 50-100 per quarter.
